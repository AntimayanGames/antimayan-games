MAYAN BEGINNINGS

(Inspiration Stage, 15 ACE)
Although the Mayan Washaka (ネラマ) existed, the Mayans as a powerful entity only came into existence
during the Inspiration Stage of the universe.
The Mayans started out as a small army, ruled by the Washaka. They soon grew in size and power on the
opposite side of the universe, far away from the Forbidden One.

THE ZAIN

(Inspiration Stage, 145 ACE)
An internal conflict arose between the Restu and the Stadu of the Mayans, two similar but different groups in
Mayan culture.
Restu Mayans believed that divine supremacy (total control over the universe) lied inside the Forbidden One,
only obtainable with the use of brute force.
Stadu Mayans believed the Forbidden One was a distraction to their divine supremacy as univese leader,
claiming that if they focused on conquering one Land at a time they would eventually reach divine supremacy
without bloodshed.
This divide led to The Zain, a civil war between the two Mayan groups. The Restu Mayans ended up winning the war, but
not without consequences.

HENRE'S WRATH

For ages, the Mayans have been led by the fearless Washaka, a symbol of strength and bravery to many native Mayans who
lived during the first Reckoning.
The untold side of this well-known legend lies in the Goron society, a society that broke off from the Mayan empire long
before the Mayan Washaka was conceived.
Henre is the decendant of an elite Mayan family that has considerable wealth and power among the Mayans. Born a Goron,
few Mayans-- or for that matter, beings in general knew of his existence in this plane.
In an effort to make himself known, he travelled to the Mayan civilization and had a child. Henre had to sacrifice his
physical form to create his child, which explains his existence as a metaphysical eyeball.
His child, of course, is the Mayan Washaka.
He succeeded in getting well known around the Mayan empire, and his name was reportedly recorded and mentioned in many
works of literature during the early years of the Washaka. Unfortunately, many records and books written during this
time were either burned or lost in translation.
Henre, being an immortal being, became enraged with the Mayan legacy left behind for him. Most Mayans looked towards the
Mayan Washaka for guidance towards the Forbidden One, and they left behind the ideas that the Henre presented. Left with
a thirst for vengeance, he succeeded in overthrowing the Mayan government, and formed the Mayan Temple.
